# OAP Contest Manager — User Guide

A quick guide to generating your UIL One-Act Play contest documents. No install, no
files to manage — just a website you sign in to.

---

## 1. Open the app and sign in

Go to **https://allenotto.com/oapmanager** (or directly to
`https://oapcmgenerator-front-end-production.up.railway.app`).

Sign in one of two ways:
- **Continue with Google** — one tap, nothing to remember.
- **Email me a link** — enter your email, we send you a one-time sign-in link; open
  it and you're in. No password ever.

> **First time / "Google hasn't verified this app" screen:** while the app is in
> early access you may see a Google warning. Tap **Advanced → Go to OAP Contest
> Manager** to continue. (You need to be added as an approved user first — ask the
> contest manager.) If a sign-in email doesn't arrive, check your spam/promotions
> folder.

Works on your phone, tablet, or computer. You can sign in on more than one device —
your contests follow you.

## 2. Your dashboard

After signing in you land on a list of your contests, each showing its name and when
you last edited it (e.g. *"2026 — 5A District 20 OAP · last edited 2 days ago"*).
From here you can:

- **Create** a new contest from scratch (a new season or level).
- **Open** a contest to edit it.
- **Duplicate** last year's contest to roll it forward — schools, directors, and your
  CM info carry over, so you're not retyping stable data.
- **Delete** a contest you no longer need.

## 3. Fill in your contest

Inside a contest, you fill the same information as always, organized into sections
you expand one at a time, with progress indicators so you can see what's done:

1. **CM Info** — your name, contact details (pre-filled once and remembered).
2. **Contest Identity** — year, classification, level, host school/venue.
3. **Contest Details** — contest date, meeting/first-show times, judging format,
   rehearsals, fees, deadlines, and your Speechwire login.
4. **Adjudicators** — your judges' names and mailing addresses.
5. **Schools & Directors** — each school, its director(s), and email(s).
6. **Play Titles & Performance Order** — each school's play and draw order.

**There is no Save button.** Every keystroke is saved instantly in your browser and
synced to your account in the background whenever you're online. Refresh, close the
tab, switch computers — your work is there.

### Handy tools while you work
- **Schedule Preview** updates live as you type times and add schools, so you can
  sanity-check the day's timing before generating anything.
- **Email Composer** drafts your routine emails (announcement, deadline reminder,
  day-before, judges, advancing schools) filled from your contest data — one
  copy-paste away.
- **Critique Randomizer** assigns judges to critiques following UIL rules; you can
  **lock** an assignment so it survives regeneration, and reorder by hand if needed.

## 4. Checkpoints (save a moment you can return to)

At any point you can save a named **checkpoint** with a note — *"before judge
change"*, *"final draw"* — and later restore it from the contest's version history.
Think of it as a labeled bookmark of your whole contest at that instant, so you can
safely undo a bad round of edits.

## 5. Generate your documents

When you're ready, open **Documents to Generate**, check the ones you want, and click
**Generate** to download a single ZIP. The full set (14 documents) includes your
checklists, agendas, schedules, letters, scripts, contact list, adjudicator info
sheet, adjudicator ballot packets, and timer form — the same documents you rely on,
in the same formats (they open cleanly in Word, Excel, and Google Drive).

A few notes:
- **Adjudicator Packets (PDF)** and the **Timer form** are **unchecked by default**.
  Check Adjudicator Packets only once your judges are set — it fills the official UIL
  ballots and takes a moment longer to build.
- Before generating, you'll get **warnings** for likely mistakes (missing date,
  duplicate performance order, etc.) — you can fix them or proceed anyway.
- Every ZIP also contains a small **contest file** (a `.json`) — a portable backup you
  can re-import from the dashboard to restore or hand off a contest on any machine.
  (This replaces the old "Regenerate.html".)

## 6. Contest day / no wifi

Once the app has loaded on a device, it works **fully offline** — viewing, editing,
and generating documents all work with no internet, so a theatre with dead wifi is
no problem. Any edits you make offline sync automatically the next time you're
online. You can also "install" the app to your home screen for one-tap access.

## 7. A few good-to-knows

- **Your Speechwire login stays on your device only.** It's clearly labeled as such
  and is never sent to the server.
- **Always the latest version.** Because it's a website, you never update anything —
  though if you have it open when a new version ships, you'll see a small **"new
  version available"** prompt; click it when you're between tasks.
- **Something look off in a document?** The generated files reproduce the long-standing
  formats exactly. If a value is wrong, it's almost always a field in the contest data
  — fix it and regenerate.

---

That's it. Sign in, fill it in, click Generate. Welcome aboard.
